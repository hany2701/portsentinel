1\. Executive Summary: The "Agentic" Shift   
In 2026, the global supply chain has moved beyond simple digitization to Autonomous Orchestration. Traditional "data silos" between procurement, warehousing, and logistics are being bridged by Agentic AI—systems that do not just provide information, but reason through complex trade-offs and suggest proactive interventions.   
Your task is to build a functional Digital Orchestrator. This agent must synthesize real-time data to solve a specific bottleneck in a modern Digital Supply Chain (DSC) environment. 

By the end of this assignment, students will be able to:   
• a) Evaluate the architecture of LLM-based agents in an industrial context.   
• b) Build a functional web-based chatbot using rapid "vibecoding" tools (e.g., claude code,   
antigravity, codex, Lovable, Bolt.new, Replit Agent, etc.).   
• c) Integrate state-of-the-art APIs (e.g., Gemini 3/2.5 Pro, DeepSeek-V4, Claude 3.5 Sonnet,   
etc.).   
• d) Design and simulate a professional supply chain interaction for a specific persona.   
• e) Critically reflect on the trade-offs between "hallucination risk" and "operational agility" in   
AI-driven logistics.

The Tuas Mega Port Resilience Monitor:   
An agent that monitors real-time maritime disruptions (e.g., weather in the Malacca Strait or automated berth congestion at Tuas) and suggests immediate re-routing or inventory safety-stock adjustments.
